# Privacy Policy

Dolmadang Soft (hereinafter "Company") places great importance on the protection of personal information provided by users for service use and complies with the Personal Information Protection Act.

Through this Privacy Policy, the Company provides specific information on how personal information disclosed by users is used, for what purposes, and what measures are taken to protect personal information.

This Privacy Policy may be changed in accordance with changes in relevant laws and regulations and changes in the services provided by the Company. Please check it regularly when using the app.

This Privacy Policy applies only to the **Aura** service provided by the Company.

---

## 1. Purpose of Processing Personal Information

The Company collects personal information for the use of AI face reading and palm reading analysis services and for service improvement through statistics and analysis, and may use it to analyze the distribution, interests, and usage patterns of data subjects.

The specific collection and use purposes for each item of personal information collected by the Company are as follows:

| Item | Category | Content | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Face/Palm Reading Analysis | Non-login (free) | Face/hand images (processed on-device; uploaded only when saving) | Analysis is performed only on-device |
| Face/Palm Reading Analysis | Login (save results) | Email address, display name (provided via Google/Kakao OAuth) | Required for saving and retrieving results |
| Error Inquiry | - | Email address | Required for submitting and responding to error inquiries |

---

## 2. Retention Period for Personal Information

The Company retains personal information collected from users for the purpose of storing analysis results and destroys it without delay upon membership withdrawal. However, it may be retained for the period specified in the following cases.

Personal information may be retained when there is a need for preservation pursuant to the provisions of laws such as the Commercial Act.

1. Records on display and advertising: 6 months (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce)
2. Records on contracts or withdrawal of subscription: 5 years (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce)
3. Records on payment and supply of goods: 5 years (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce)

---

## 3. Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties

The Company does not provide personal information to other individuals, companies, or organizations without the consent of the user or a legal basis.

**1) Provision with User Consent**

When the Company needs to share personal information to provide services in cooperation with affiliated companies or to conduct events, it notifies data subjects in advance of the joint collection or sharing of personal information and obtains consent.

**2) Cases Where Personal Information May Be Provided Without User Consent**

1. When necessary for billing in connection with service provision
2. When a warrant or equivalent official document is received from an administrative agency or investigative agency for administrative or investigative purposes in accordance with law
3. When an imminent danger to the life or safety of a user is confirmed

Please note that external services linked from the app may collect your personal information. In such cases, this Privacy Policy does not apply.

## 5. Rights and Obligations of Data Subjects and How to Exercise Them

Data subjects may exercise the following personal information protection rights against the Company at any time. Rights may be exercised through written documents, phone, or email, and the Company will take action without delay.

1. Request to access personal information
2. Request for correction if there are errors
3. Request for deletion
4. Request to suspend processing

If a data subject requests correction or deletion of errors in personal information, the Company will not use or provide the relevant personal information until the correction or deletion is completed.

Rights may be exercised through a legal representative or an authorized agent of the data subject. In this case, a power of attorney in accordance with Attached Form No. 11 of the Enforcement Rules of the Personal Information Protection Act must be submitted.

Data subjects must not violate the Personal Information Protection Act or other relevant laws and regulations to infringe upon the personal information and privacy of themselves or others being processed by the Company.

---

## 6. Procedures and Methods for Destroying Personal Information

Personal information of data subjects is, in principle, destroyed without delay once the purpose of collection and use of personal information is achieved. The Company's procedures and methods for destroying personal information are as follows:

**1) Destruction Procedure**

Information entered by data subjects for service use is immediately destroyed for non-login users after the purpose is achieved, and for login users, it is transferred to a separate database and stored for a certain period in accordance with the membership withdrawal policy and information protection reasons under relevant laws before being destroyed. Such personal information is not used for any purpose other than storage unless required by law.

**2) Destruction Method**

- Personal information stored in electronic file format is deleted using technical methods that prevent the records from being reproduced.

---

## 7. Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Information

**1) Password Encryption**

Users' personal information is protected by passwords.

**2) Management of User ID and Password**

Only the user themselves should know the password for their account, and the password must not be disclosed to anyone. It is also advisable to log out of the app after finishing. This is especially important when sharing a device with others or using it in a public place to prevent personal information from being disclosed to others.

The Company is not responsible for problems caused by the leakage of personal information such as passwords due to the user's own negligence, or for incidents caused by the inherent risks of the internet.
